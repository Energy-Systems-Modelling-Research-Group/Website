Resources

For prospective PhD students

We typically admit 1 to 3 new PhD students annually. Due to the high volume of inquiries, it is not always possible to respond to individual questions. If you are interested in our research, please apply online. The application process is detailed below.

PhD positions are usually funded, covering UK tuition fees and providing a tax-free stipend. Note that the difference between UK and international tuition fees is significant (approximately £75k over a 3-year PhD). Applications can be submitted through the Department’s Research Opportunities website via the ‘Apply?’ tab on the right-hand side of the page. You can find the link here: Strathclyde EEE PhD Applications.

Electrical Grid Updates

Some updates we've noticed from ESO or about the electrical grid in general.

  • Beyond 2030 is the ESO's plan for the next few decades, including new lines they're planning on building.
  • GB scheduling and dispatch- a case for change (a report to ESO) looks at improvements that could be made to the current setup with ESO.
  • BBC's article on Wind farm operators agreeing to pay £33m for excessive charging shows an example of operators misrepresenting themselves to Ofgem.

Maths

Most of us need to study a bit more maths during our PhDs, so we've started a list of resources we've used to help us learn.

  • For general maths concepts, Richarde Borcherds or 3Blue1Brown on YouTube
  • For hypercubes, The Hypercube: Projections and Slicing Simulationand Spinning Hypercubes are both great simulations.
